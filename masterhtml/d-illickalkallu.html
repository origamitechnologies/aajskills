Illickal Kallu

Marvel at the majestic beauty of the Black stone standing tall and impressive facing west,  Illickal Kallu, Shrouded in mist mostly in the day, creates a floating rock of the splendid natural structure. From here the Arabian Sea can be seen in the distant horizon as a thin blue line. We can also view both the sunrise and sunset from illickal Kallu Hill Stations.

Illickal Kallu in Kottayam District is a famous hill station and the hills there has a special shape. One hill shape matches a mushroom owing to which it gets its name “Kuda Kallu”. In Malayalam Kuda Kallu means a umbrella shaped rock. Three hills, each rising to 4000 ft. and above, together form this huge hill. The medicinal herb “Neela Koduveli” grows in Illickal Kallu hills. The Neela Koduveli flowers is considered to have super-natural powers that could step-up wealth & guarantee a prosperous harvest. The second hill in Illickal Kallu is known as Kunnu Kallu as it has a small hunch on the sides. There is a half feet wide bridge called Narakapalam across the rock. We can see as a thin blue line the Arabian Sea from Illickal Kallu hilltops. We can also view both the sunrise and sunset from illickal Kallu Hill Stations.
