Ilaveezhapoonchira

Mile and a half of trekking through the clouds on the cascading mountains of Ilaveezhapoonchira, situated at 3,200 feet above from the sea level is a stunning experience lasting for lifetime.

Elaveezha poonchira is a beautiful hill station situated in Melukavu panchayat in Meenachil Taluk of Kottayam district with a long boundary with dukki district. At an elevation of 3,200 Sq. feet from the sea level with an exceptionally beautiful landscape, the stretch of land approximately 1500 meter in length offer stunning scenic views from all around.

It is believed that the name that means “the pond of flowers where no leaves fall”, is derived from a pond existed there in which no leaves would fall because of the strong wind blown always across the valley. There is yet another explanation that it was simply because no trees were in the near vicinity that no leaves had fallen in the lake of bygone era.
Whatever is the origin of the name, the sheer vastness of the valley dotted by shrubs and enveloped by the silence of the hillocks of Mankunnu, Kudayathur hill and Thonippara that guard the valley together create a spell bounding scene.

From the top of the hills which is at 3200 ft above sea level, one can view large parts of Idukki, Kottayam, Ernakulam, Alapuzha, Pathanamthitta and Thrissur districts. In some months, with proper sunlight and minimum haze, the distant white sparkling of waves beating across the seashore of Allap­puzha also is visible.
